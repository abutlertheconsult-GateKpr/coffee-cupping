What is Cupping?

Cupping is the universal industry standard for evaluating coffee quality — used by roasters, green coffee buyers, and Q Graders worldwide. It creates a controlled, repeatable tasting environment so any two people can compare notes on the same bean. The SCA protocol scores coffees on a 100-point scale; 80+ points = Specialty Grade.

Step-by-Step Process
01
Prepare Your Setup
Use 8.25g of coffee per 150ml of filtered water. Weigh on a 0.1g scale. You need ceramic cupping bowls, a burr grinder, a gooseneck kettle, and deep cupping spoons. Prepare at least 3 cups of the same bean to check consistency.
02
Grind Fresh
Grind coarser than drip, finer than French press — like coarse table salt. Grind immediately before brewing. Let grounds rest 5–10 min before adding water to allow CO₂ to off-gas.
03
Dry Aroma
Before adding water, nose the dry grounds closely. Note fruity, floral, nutty, chocolatey, or spicy notes. Cup your hand over the bowl to concentrate aromas.
04
Add Water & Bloom
Pour water at 200°F in a circular motion, fully saturating all grounds. Start your timer. A crust will form — do not disturb it. At 4 minutes evaluate the wet aroma.
05
Break the Crust
At exactly 4 minutes, push the crust back with three slow strokes. Keep your nose directly over the bowl — this is the most aromatic moment of the cupping.
06
Skim & Wait
Remove foam and floating grounds. Let cool to ~158°F — about 8–10 min from pour. The ideal tasting window is 149–113°F. Too hot dulls your palate.
07
Taste & Slurp
Load your cupping spoon and slurp aggressively — this aerates the coffee across your entire palate. Swirl around your mouth. Evaluate: flavor, aftertaste, acidity, body, balance, sweetness.
08
Taste as It Cools
Return to each cup every few minutes. Flavors change dramatically — acids brighten, sweetness clarifies, defects emerge. A great cup stays pleasant even when cold.
Scoring Scale (1–10)
The full 1–10 scale is used. 1 = undrinkable, 10 = extraordinary.
