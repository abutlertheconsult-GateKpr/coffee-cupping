What is Cupping?

Cupping is the universal industry standard for evaluating coffee quality — used by roasters, green coffee buyers, and Q Graders worldwide. It creates a controlled, repeatable tasting environment so any two people can compare notes on the same bean. The SCA protocol scores coffees on a 100-point scale; 80+ points = Specialty Grade.

Step-by-Step Process
01
Prepare Your Setup
Use 8.25g of coffee per 150ml of filtered water. Weigh precisely on a 0.1g scale. You need ceramic cupping bowls (150–200ml), a burr grinder, a gooseneck kettle, and deep cupping spoons. Prepare at least 3 cups of the same bean to check consistency.
02
Grind Fresh
Grind coarser than drip, finer than French press — like coarse table salt. Grind immediately before brewing. Let grounds rest 5–10 minutes before adding water to allow CO₂ to off-gas and fragrance to develop.
03
Dry Aroma
Before adding water, nose the dry grounds closely. Note fruity, floral, nutty, chocolatey, or spicy notes. This is your first scoring moment: Fragrance. Cup your hand over the bowl to concentrate aromas.
04
Add Water & Bloom
Pour water at 200°F in a steady circular motion, fully saturating all grounds. Start your timer. A crust will form. Do not disturb it. At 4 minutes, evaluate the wet aroma before breaking the crust.
05
Break the Crust
At exactly 4 minutes, push the crust back with three slow strokes. Keep your nose directly over the bowl — this is the most aromatic moment of the cupping. Score your Aroma impression now.
06
Skim & Wait
Remove foam and floating grounds. Let cool to ~158°F — about 8–10 minutes from pour. Tasting too hot dulls your palate. The ideal tasting window is 149–113°F.
07
Taste & Slurp
Load your cupping spoon and slurp aggressively — this aerates the coffee across your entire palate. Don’t sip politely. Swirl it around your mouth. Spit or swallow. Evaluate: flavor, aftertaste, acidity, body, balance, sweetness.
08
Taste as It Cools
Return to each cup every few minutes as it cools. Flavors change dramatically — acids brighten, sweetness clarifies, defects become more apparent. A great cup stays pleasant even cold.

Pro tip: Always cup blind when possible — cover the bean labels until after scoring. Bias is real. Even experienced Q Graders are influenced by origin expectations. Rest roasted beans 8–24 hours before cupping; freshly roasted coffee is still degassing and will taste flat or grassy.
